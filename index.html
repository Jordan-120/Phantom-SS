Cobalt SS/TC Description

Only 3,168 Cobalt SS Turbo Coupes were ever manufactured, making it one of the rarest vehicles produced by GM. This was GM's entry into the tuner market, shifting from the supercharged LSJ to the turbocharged LNF engine in 2007. The car also included a 24 mm front stabilizer bar, 22 mm rear stabilizer bar, F35-tuned suspension, and a limited-slip differential. Due to this switch, the Cobalt SS/TC holds the record for the fastest front-wheel drive production car lap at the Nürburgring Nordschleife, clocking 8:22.85. This vehicle prioritized performance over appearance, resulting in limited mainstream appeal. Production ended in 2010.
